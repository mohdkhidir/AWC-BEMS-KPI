■ Key Insights & Analysis
NIH — Prolonged Deduction
NIH carries old work orders from 2022 (e.g. NIHB002526 Gas Chromatography dated Oct 2022). These unresolved WOs repeatedly trigger B2 deductions each month, contributing RM 159,740 (79%) of total portfolio deductions.
HOAG — B2 Escalating Trend
HOAG B2 deductions trended up Jan→Mar (RM1,140→RM2,200) driven by Dental Equipment failures. ADEC Chair (10108, 10132) appeared in 3 consecutive months suggesting a chronic maintenance gap. Apr recovered strongly to RM200.
IKN — Best Managed Site
IKN consistently achieves 99.75–99.93% performance. Despite managing high-value CT Scanners (RM4.6M), deduction rate never exceeds 0.25%. Strong PPM discipline and rapid response.
B2 Dominates — 60% of Deductions
Unscheduled repair (B2) accounts for RM 129,330 (60.4%) of all deductions. Root causes: Battery/power issues (22%), Equipment failure (28%), Component wear (19%). Preventive replacement programme recommended.
HSA — Uptime (B4) Trend Rising
HSA B4 deductions increased from RM620 (Jan) to RM2,250 (Apr). This signals growing uptime compliance issues. If unchecked, Apr trend may continue into Q2 2026.
NIH PPM Compliance Improving
NIH B3 deduction reduced from RM10,600 (Jan) to RM10 (Apr) — a 99.9% improvement. This indicates the PPM backlog is being cleared. However B2 repair remains stubbornly high at RM23,630–27,000/month.
