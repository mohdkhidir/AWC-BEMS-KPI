■ B2 Prolonged Deduction & B3 PPM — Key Insights
⚠ NIH — Prolonged B2 Deduction (Critical)
NIH carries old work orders from 2022 (Gas Chromatography NIHB002526, dated Oct 2022 — 4× recurrence). These unresolved WOs trigger B2 deductions every month, accounting for RM 92,130 alone. NIH is the biggest contributor at RM 159,740 (73.8%) of total portfolio deductions.
🔧 HOAG — B2 Escalation & Recovery
HOAG B2 deductions escalated Jan→Mar (RM1,140→RM2,200) driven by Dental Chair failures (ADEC 10108 & 10132, 3 consecutive months). Apr recovered to RM200. May achieved zero deduction (100% performance) — indicating the dental equipment issue was resolved.
⭐ IKN — Best Managed Site
IKN consistently achieves 99.75–99.93% performance. Managing high-value CT Scanners (RM4.6M) and Immunoassay Analyzers, deduction rate never exceeds 1.00%. Strong PPM discipline, rapid response, and disciplined asset management model.
📈 B2 Dominates — 65.9% of All Deductions
Unscheduled repair (B2) accounts for RM 142,610 (65.9%) of all deductions. Root causes: Equipment Failure (28%), Battery/Power (22%), Component Wear (19%). Recommendation: Preventive Replacement Programme for battery-dependent devices.
📈 HSA — B4 Uptime Rising Trend
HSA B4 deductions increased from RM620 (Jan) to RM2,250 (Apr) — a 263% rise. Signals growing uptime compliance issues. If trend continues into May–June, HSA could drop below the 95% target. Uptime monitoring and escalation protocol required.
📋 NIH B3 PPM — Strong Recovery
NIH B3 deduction reduced from RM10,600 (Jan) to RM10 (Apr) — a 99.9% improvement. PPM backlog is being cleared. However B2 repair remains stubbornly high at RM23,630–27,000/month, driven by laboratory analyser chronic failures. Structured overhaul plan needed.
